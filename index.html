Artificial Intelligent- The next face of Online education

Online leaning all of a sudden has become the front and center as schools, parents, and students progressed from traditional education to online learning platforms. In the coming future, everyone is going to be forced to get better at using e-learning tools but is that enough? Artificial intelligence (AI), the technology that can help change the face of online education platform completely by supporting the challenges that teachers are facing while trying to teach remotely.


Remote learning relies on the self-motivated and self-interested fully-fledged learner, who knows using the platform and techniques specially designed for corporates and the higher-education market. Each child has different needs, and access to inspiring teachers and content to keep them engaged is of paramount importance. AI technology can provide the kind of resources that every child expects at the time of studying online resulting in significantly improved learner outcomes in the long-term. Most teachers don’t have enough time in front of their students where they can focus on their specific needs. AI helps to free up this time to allow teachers to focus on students and topics where their time is most needed and valuable. All we need is machine learning techniques to create a learning profile where teachers can view the reviews of students, submission, and performance automatically. AI can use these insights to get to know about the interests of students and recommend specific learning content pathways that help the students to develop their knowledge. Multiple companies can leverage AI and analytics which helps teachers to mark work and provide useful feedback once the student completes his learning content. Students can take a picture of their finished assignments and submit them. Teachers can see the progress of the content and which areas the student is struggling with more and need to revisit. Most importantly, AI can be used to help update the national curriculum by tracking the performance and continuously learning from its interactions with the students.

This period of unexpected closure and forced e-learning is an opportunity for schools to get comfortable with the new technology. And when students and teachers go back to school, they’re going to take many new digital tools and techniques back with them. These will form the foundations for more digital education for future generations.

Face Recognition Based Attendance System

A system that checks that takes attendance of an Employee and checks if an employee is on time or has left early. This system can be used as an alternative to the Biometric fingerprint.



We can place a camera at the entrance which will recognize the employee and save the logs to a database which has their entry and exit time.With the help of the user logs and a few conditions, we can find if they are late for work or if they have left early.

Data Processing


As soon as the camera detects the employee it will check if there are any records for that particular employee and if so, it will time and date is assigned to it. If the employee is detected first time today, arrival time will be assigned, once this time is determined, each detection on the same day will update his departure time.


Implementation at Large Scale


Facial recognition is commonly used in law enforcement where a database of images of known


Criminals can be searched, and the relevant authorities notified of a match.


Facial recognition is also commonly used for enterprise-wide security. In facial recognition security systems, a database of employee facial images can be searched, and the employee is only allowed access to a restricted area (either physical or virtual) on a successful match.


● A recent report out of China stated that a school has installed facial recognition technology in its classrooms to monitor students’ behavior (Source: BusinessInsider).

● At the end of May 2018, the US Homeland Security Science and Technology Directorate

Published the results of sponsored tests at the Maryland Test Facility (MdTF) done in

March. These real-life tests measured the performance of 12 face recognition systems in a corridor measuring 2 m by 2.5 m.

● Thales’ solution utilizing Facial recognition software (LFIS) achieved excellent results with a face acquisition rate of 99.44% in less than 5 seconds (against an average of 68%), a Vendor True Identification Rate of 98% in less than 5 seconds compared with an average 66%, and an error rate of 1% compared with an average of 32%.

The Future of Education Post Corona



The widespread of COVID-19 has surrounded the entire world with a cloud of uncertainty looming over the future after this pandemic. It is no news; it has affected various sectors and the day to day life of the people. As we see the situation keeps on worsening as the days pass and we see a rise in adverse impact of the education industry.

To begin with, all major examinations like entrance tests for different streams, boards and final examinations have been postponed, creating anxiety and stress among students and parents as well as the faculty of respective institutions. The lockdown and panic surrounding the current crisis has certainly decreased the number of registrations for admission for the coming year.

To combat these problems and benefit the students, education institutions can adopt and integrate emerging technology driven tools. This adaptation will definitely change the dynamics of the education system for betterment.

The change will be at a slower pace for education institutions due to centuries old lecture-based approach in classroom-oriented space. However, this virus has surely been a catalyst for education industry to look out for various innovative ways to stay connected and facilitate services.

As we move forward to inculcate new normal in our everyday life, many professionals from different industries have come together to develop and advance technologies with artificial intelligence, blockchain, cybersecurity, data science, machine learning and many more for user friendly approach.

Here are some benefits of technology driven education system

· Enhanced learning with industry experts

· Lower Costs

· Secured and verified transfer of documents

· Generate automated database

· Easier to track academic performance

· Flexible learning

· Better communication

· Easier accessibility

· Time and location flexibility

· User-friendly approach

· Mitigate unforeseen circumstances

· Encourage innovative and creative solution-based thinking

· Easier to adapt technological changes in future

The spread of COVID-19 reminds us of our resilience to face the threats and unpredictable situations. This time is an opportunity to shift focus on digitalization and the skills needed for this rapidly changing environment. This could be the silver lining in the dark clouds, education and technology together can be a head start to a brighter future which will ensure learning never stops.

New National Educational Policy



In 2014, India’s global education ranking slipped to 93. This, together with a series of scams faced by the Indian education sector, calls for an immediate need to bring reforms in our education system. That’s when, in July 2020, Union Cabinet cleared a new National Education Policy (NEP) proposing extensive changes in school and higher education. The NEP is a comprehensive framework to lead the development of education in the country. The NEP proposes some of the panoramic changes including opening up of Indian higher education to foreign universities, dismantling of the UGC, and the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), the introduction of a 4-year multidisciplinary undergraduate program with multiple exit options, and discontinuation of M Phil program. Let us look in detail what the new NEP offers:


School Education:


Ø There’s going to be a new Pedagogical and curricular structure which means that the current 10+2 system to be replaced by a new 5+3+3+4 with foundation stage, preparatory stage, middle stage, and secondary stage curricular structure corresponding to ages 3–8, 8–11, 11–14, 14–18 years respectively.

Ø Class 10 and 12 board examinations to be made easier. Will focus on core competencies rather than memorizing a huge amount of facts. Following that if he/she is not satisfied with their marks that they scored they will be able to take the exam again.

Ø Vocational education starts from class 6 with internships. Students will be given exposure to the industry and will be learning new programming languages as a part of 21st-century skills.

Ø Teaching up to at least class 5 to be in regional language. No language will be imposed on any student.

Ø School governance is set to change, with a new accreditation framework and an independent authority to regulate both public and private schools.

Ø Universalization of education from preschool to secondary level with 100% Gross Enrollment Ratio (GER) in school education by 2030.

Ø No rigid separation between Arts and Science streams and curricular and extra-curricular activities in school education and higher education. For instance, if a student wants to pursue fashion studies with Physics, or if one wants to learn bakery with Chemistry, they will be allowed to do so. An arts student could now take up Physics or Mathematics as a subject while a Science student could take up Music as a subject.

Higher Education:

Ø Introduction of new policy envisages broad-based, multi-disciplinary, holistic UG education with a flexible curriculum, creative combinations of subjects, integration of vocational education, and multiple entries and exit points with appropriate certification.

Ø UG education can now be of 3–4 years with multiple exit options and appropriate certification within this period. This means, Certificate after 1 year, Advanced Diploma after 2 years, Bachelor’s Degree after 3 years and Bachelor’s with Research after 4 years.

Ø Higher education institutes will be transformed into large, well resourced, vibrant institutes providing high-quality teaching, research, and community engagement.

Ø Affiliation of colleges is to be phased out in 15 years and the state-wise mechanism is to be established for granting graded autonomy.

Ø All professional education will be an integral part of the higher education system. Stand-alone technical universities, health science universities, and other legal, agriculture universities, etc. will aim to become multi-disciplinary institutions.

Ø the National Educational Technology Forum (NETF), will be created to provide a platform for the free exchange of ideas on the use of technology to enhance learning, assessment, planning, administration. Appropriate integration of technology into all levels of education will be done to improve classroom processes, support teacher professional development, enhance educational access for disadvantaged groups and streamline educational planning, administration, and management.

Ø M.Phil. courses will be discontinued and all the courses at undergraduate, postgraduate and Ph.D. level will now be interdisciplinary.

Learning comes under pressure


In recent years, colleges and universities have come under increasing pressure as costs have risen. This increase is due to investments in inexpensive technology, teachers’ salaries, and administrative costs. While governments have confirmed that they can no longer afford to subsidize their educational efforts as generously as they used to.

As employers move from degree-based hiring to competency-based hiring, many will determine that degrees are not a priority or even required for certain jobs. Over the next few years, degrees are likely to become MIA in many job descriptions and this will lead an increasing number of students to seek post-secondary education bundles that are shorter, less expensive, and more clearly connected to careers or even specific employers.” — Ryan Craig, Managing Partner at University Ventures.

Corporate learning goes online


For global organizations, digital transformation is high on the agenda of senior executives looking to compete in the digital economy. Due to the pace of digital, technologies, and channels change at an alarming rate, and to keep up employees need to continuously learn to stay ahead. For many organizations, online learning provides a flexible and simple way to up skill staff in a scalable way.

The future of online learning


While classroom learning is still valued in today’s modern world, many people no longer need or want to be tied to a particular place or time to learn new skills. In fact, according to the National Center for Education Statistics, in the US, around 25% of all college and university students are now enrolled in distance education courses.

As a result, an increasing number of institutions are offering flexible online learning solutions in addition to their traditional syllabuses, while smaller schools and universities are expected to offer almost entirely online-based courses in the not so distant future. In addition to the projected growth of mobile learning products and the corporate learning and development sector, the future for online learning appears dazzling bright.

-Naresh Nishad
